# CE Deep Research Discovery Prompt

**Purpose:** Comprehensive research brief for NotebookLM or deep research tools. Execute this before running the CE Research Agent pipeline to gather foundational intelligence.

---

## Instructions for NotebookLM

Upload relevant source documents to your notebook, then use the following questions to extract structured insights across all context engineering dimensions.

---

## 1. Company Intelligence

### Core Identity
- What is the company's official name, founding date, and headquarters location?
- Who are the founders and what are their backgrounds?
- What is the company's mission statement and stated vision?
- What stage is the company at (seed, Series A/B/C, public)?
- What is their funding history and current valuation if known?

### Product & Offering
- What is the core product or service?
- What are the key features and capabilities?
- How does the product technically work (architecture, tech stack)?
- What are the different pricing tiers and plans?
- What integrations or partnerships are available?

### Proof & Traction
- What customers or logos can be identified?
- What case studies or success stories exist?
- What metrics or outcomes do they claim (ROI, time saved, etc.)?
- What awards, certifications, or recognitions have they received?
- What is their team size and growth trajectory?

---

## 2. Competitor Intelligence

### Competitive Landscape
- Who are the top 5-7 direct competitors?
- Who are the indirect competitors or alternative solutions?
- How do customers currently solve this problem without this product?

### Per-Competitor Analysis
For each major competitor, gather:
- Company overview and positioning
- Core product differences
- Pricing comparison
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Target audience overlap
- Recent news or strategic moves

### Differentiation
- What does this company do that competitors don't?
- What is their unique technical approach?
- Where do they lose deals and why?

---

## 3. Market Intelligence

### Market Definition
- What market category does the company operate in?
- What are the adjacent markets?
- How is the market typically segmented?

### Market Size & Dynamics
- What is the TAM/SAM/SOM (if available)?
- What is the market growth rate?
- What are the key market drivers?
- What regulatory or compliance factors affect the market?

### Trends & Shifts
- What macro trends are shaping this market?
- What technology shifts are relevant (AI, cloud, etc.)?
- What are the emerging threats or disruptors?
- What do industry analysts say about the space?

### Contrarian Insights
- What does the market believe that might be wrong?
- What hidden opportunities exist?
- What problems are underserved?

---

## 4. Audience Intelligence

### Target Segments
- Who are the primary buyer personas (roles, titles)?
- What company types are ideal customers (size, industry, stage)?
- What are the firmographic characteristics?
- Who are the economic buyers vs. end users?

### Jobs to Be Done
- What are they trying to accomplish before they find this product?
- What does success look like for them?
- What triggers the search for a solution?

### Pains & Frustrations
- What are their daily operational pains?
- What are their strategic fears and risks?
- What have they tried that didn't work?
- What do they complain about in reviews or forums?

### Desires & Outcomes
- What transformation do they want?
- What would make them a hero in their organization?
- What emotional outcomes do they seek?

### Objections & Barriers
- What stops them from buying?
- What concerns do they raise in sales conversations?
- What friction exists in adoption?

### Language & Voice
- What phrases do they use to describe their problems?
- What terminology is native to them?
- What sources do they trust?

---

## 5. Brand & Voice Intelligence

### Current Brand Expression
- How does the company describe itself on the website?
- What tone do they use in marketing materials?
- What messaging themes appear repeatedly?

### Voice Characteristics
- Formal vs. casual?
- Technical vs. accessible?
- Bold vs. humble?
- Urgent vs. patient?

### Messaging Pillars
- What are the 3-5 core messages?
- What taglines or slogans do they use?
- What metaphors or analogies appear?

### Visual & Experiential Brand
- What does the visual identity communicate?
- What is the user experience like?
- How do they show up at events or in community?

---

## 6. Strategy Intelligence

### Business Model
- How do they make money?
- What is the pricing strategy?
- What is the customer acquisition model?

### Go-to-Market
- What channels do they use for acquisition?
- What is their content strategy?
- How do they approach outbound vs. inbound?
- What partnerships are strategic?

### Growth Loops
- What viral or network effects exist?
- What retention mechanisms are in place?
- How do customers expand usage over time?

### AI & Technology Strategy
- How is AI integrated into the product?
- What is their AI differentiation?
- What governance or safety measures exist?
- What is their technical roadmap?

### Strategic Bets
- What big bets are they making?
- What are they saying no to?
- Where are they investing?

---

## 7. Content & Positioning Intelligence

### Content Audit
- What content types do they produce (blog, video, podcast)?
- What topics dominate their content?
- What content performs best?

### SEO & Discovery
- What keywords do they rank for?
- What search intent are they capturing?
- Where are the content gaps?

### Thought Leadership
- Who are the public voices of the company?
- What conferences or events do they speak at?
- What original research or insights do they publish?

---

## Output Format Request

After researching each dimension, please structure your findings as:

```
## [Dimension Name]

### Key Findings
- [Bullet points of most important discoveries]

### Evidence & Sources
- [Specific quotes, data points, or references]

### Gaps & Uncertainties
- [What couldn't be confirmed or needs more research]

### Implications for Context Framework
- [How this informs positioning, messaging, or strategy]
```

---

## Priority Order

If time is limited, research in this order:
1. **Company** - Foundation for everything
2. **Audience** - Who we're talking to
3. **Competitors** - Landscape understanding
4. **Market** - Context and trends
5. **Brand/Voice** - How to communicate
6. **Strategy** - How they execute

---

## Follow-Up Questions

After initial research, probe deeper with:
- "What evidence contradicts the company's claims?"
- "What do negative reviews say?"
- "What are customers saying on Reddit, Twitter, or forums?"
- "What do former employees say on Glassdoor?"
- "What recent news might change this picture?"
